Odak — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 19, 2026

Odak ("the app", "we") is a self-control / commitment tool that helps you limit your own use of apps you choose. This policy explains what data Odak handles and how. The short version: Odak has no login, no backend, and collects nothing that identifies you — no name, no email, no phone number, and nothing leaves your device at all.

What Odak stores on your device

  • The list of apps you chose to lock, and your lock/break settings.
  • Your wait-escalation state (how many times you have opened a locked app today).
  • App-usage statistics that Odak reads with your permission (Usage Access) to show you how long you use your apps. These numbers are shown to you on your device and are not sent anywhere.
  • A local record of your own activity (deterrent attempts, breaks, and any skip payments) used to show you your statistics.

Odak has no servers and no account system. There is nothing of yours stored anywhere except on your own device. We do not run a backend, we do not receive your data, and we have no crash-reporting or analytics service watching the app in the field — if it crashes for you, we only find out if you tell us.

What Odak does NOT do

  • No login, and no collection of your name, email or phone number.
  • No advertising and no ad identifiers.
  • No analytics or tracking SDKs, no crash reporting, no behavioural profiling, no selling or sharing of your data.
  • Odak does not transmit your app-usage data or your list of locked apps anywhere — there is nowhere for it to go.

Permissions Odak uses, and why

  • Usage Access (PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS): to detect when a locked app is opened and to show your usage statistics. Read on-device only; never transmitted.
  • Display over other apps (SYSTEM_ALERT_WINDOW): to show the block screen over a locked app.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS): to show the ongoing protection notification and gentle reminders.
  • Run at startup (RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED): to resume protection after you restart your device.
  • Foreground service (special use): to keep enforcement running so the block screen can appear the moment you open a locked app.

Payments

If you choose to pay to skip a wait, the payment is processed by your app store's billing system (Google Play or the Apple App Store) under that store's own terms and privacy policy. Odak does not see or store your card details. The price is the one shown to you at the time of purchase, in your store's currency. The fee is flat — it does not increase, no matter how many times you use it.

Honesty about blocking

Odak is a commitment aid, not an unbreakable lock. Because it runs as a normal Android app, blocking depends on the operating system and can be bypassed (for example by force-stopping, restarting, or uninstalling Odak). We do not prevent you from removing the app through normal Android settings.

If you reinstall Odak, your waiting time resets. Uninstalling and reinstalling Odak is a way to reset the wait-escalation state — we know, and we are not currently closing this gap, because doing so would require running a server, and we have chosen not to run one. Uninstalling removes the app and all its data from your device, and Odak never prevents you from uninstalling it.

Data retention and deletion

Everything Odak stores is on your device only. Uninstall Odak to remove it — that deletes your locked-app list, your settings, your statistics and your wait state completely. There is no server copy to request deletion of, and no account to delete.

Children

Odak is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Changes

If we change how Odak handles data (for example, if a future version adds a network feature), we will update this policy and the Play Data Safety section before that version ships.
