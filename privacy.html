Odak — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 19, 2026

This privacy policy covers Odak ("the app", "we", "us"), a self-control / commitment tool that helps you limit your own use of apps you choose. It applies to the Android version of Odak distributed through Google Play; if a version for another platform (such as iOS) is released, this policy will be updated before that version ships. Questions or requests can be sent any time to odakapp@ome-marketing.com.

The short version: Odak has no login, no account system, no backend, and collects nothing that identifies you — no name, no email address, no phone number, no device identifier, no location. Odak's own code makes no network connections at all: everything the app knows about you stays on your device, and we never receive it. The app does include Google Play's billing component so purchases can be handled by the Play Store, and that component requires internet access — but it carries none of your Odak data with it.

What Odak stores on your device

  • The list of apps you chose to lock, and your lock/break settings.
  • Your wait-escalation state (how many times you have opened a locked app today).
  • App-usage statistics that Odak reads with your permission (Usage Access) to show you how long you use your apps. These numbers are shown to you on your device and are not sent anywhere.
  • A local record of your own activity in Odak (deterrent attempts, breaks, and any skip purchases) used to show you your statistics.

Odak has no servers and no account system. There is nothing of yours stored anywhere except on your own device, in the app's private storage, which Android shields from other apps. We do not run a backend, we do not receive your data, and we have no crash-reporting or analytics service watching the app in the field — if it crashes for you, we only find out if you tell us.

What Odak does NOT do

  • No login, and no collection of your name, email address or phone number.
  • No collection of device identifiers, advertising identifiers, or location.
  • No advertising of any kind.
  • No analytics or tracking SDKs, no crash reporting, no behavioural profiling.
  • No selling of your data, no sharing of your data, and no use of your data for any purpose other than showing it back to you — Odak does not transmit your app-usage data or your list of locked apps anywhere. There is nowhere for it to go.
  • No reading of your screen content, messages, or anything inside other apps (see the Accessibility section below — the service is technically incapable of it).

Permissions Odak uses, and why

Each of these is requested in the app with an explanation before Android shows you the permission dialog, and each is used only for the purpose stated here:

  • Usage Access (PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS): to detect when a locked app is opened and to show your usage statistics. Read on-device only; never transmitted. You can revoke it at any time in Android Settings → Special app access → Usage access (Odak's blocking and statistics stop working without it).
  • Display over other apps (SYSTEM_ALERT_WINDOW): to show the block screen over a locked app. The overlay is only ever Odak's own block/wait screen — it never covers system navigation or the Settings app, and it carries no ads.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS): to show the ongoing protection notification and gentle reminders.
  • Run at startup (RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED): to resume protection after you restart your device. It restarts only Odak's own service.
  • Foreground service (special use): to keep enforcement running so the block screen can appear the moment you open a locked app.
  • Accessibility service (optional, off by default): Odak offers an optional accessibility service that closes one specific bypass: returning to an already-open locked app through the Recents screen, which the Usage Access permission alone cannot detect reliably. If you choose to enable it in Android Settings → Accessibility, the service receives only window-change events — that is, "which app just came to the foreground." It is configured so that it cannot read screen content, cannot read text you type, cannot see inside other apps, cannot perform gestures or actions on your behalf, and cannot interfere with Android Settings or with uninstalling Odak. The foreground-app information it sees is used on-device, immediately, solely to re-show the block screen you asked for, and is never stored beyond the statistics described above or transmitted anywhere. You can turn the service off at any time in the same Accessibility settings; Odak keeps working without it, minus that one detection.
  • Billing (com.android.vending.BILLING): declared so the paid skip can be offered through your app store's own billing system (see Payments). It grants no access to any data about you.
  • Internet (INTERNET): required by Google Play's own billing library, which Odak includes so that purchases can be handled by the Play Store (see Payments). Odak's own code makes no network requests and sends no data. Nothing described in "What Odak stores on your device" above is ever transmitted — the network capability exists solely because Google's billing component requires it, and you can verify that with any network monitor.

Revoking any of these permissions is always possible through Android Settings and never requires contacting us.

Payments

If you choose to pay to skip a wait, the payment is processed entirely by your app store's billing system (Google Play, or the Apple App Store on a future iOS version) under that store's own terms and privacy policy — the store is the only third party involved in Odak at all. Odak never sees or stores your card details, billing address, or store account information; it only learns "the purchase succeeded" and records that fact in your local statistics. The price is the one shown to you by the store at the time of purchase, in your store's currency. The fee is flat — it does not increase, no matter how many times you use it.

Beyond the store's billing system, Odak shares data with no third parties, embeds no third-party SDKs that collect data, and has no parent, subsidiary, or partner with access to your data — because no data ever reaches us in the first place.

Honesty about blocking

Odak is a commitment aid, not an unbreakable lock. Because it runs as a normal Android app, blocking depends on the operating system and can be bypassed (for example by force-stopping, restarting, or uninstalling Odak, or by disabling its permissions). We do not prevent you from removing the app or changing its permissions through normal Android settings, and we never will — that is your device, not ours.

If you reinstall Odak, your waiting time resets. Uninstalling and reinstalling Odak is a way to reset the wait-escalation state — we know, and we are not currently closing this gap, because doing so would require running a server and keeping a record of your device, and we have chosen to do neither.

Data security

Your data is protected primarily by never being transmitted: there is no network transfer to intercept and no server to breach. On the device, Odak keeps its data in the app's private storage area, which the Android operating system isolates from other apps. Standard Android device protections (screen lock, device encryption) apply to it like to any other app data.

Data retention and deletion

Everything Odak stores lives on your device only, and is kept only as long as the app is installed. Uninstall Odak to delete all of it — your locked-app list, your settings, your statistics and your wait state, completely and immediately. There is no server copy to request deletion of, no account to close, and nothing we could hand over, restore, or retain even if asked. Your purchase history with the app store is held by the store under its own policy, not by us.

Your privacy rights (GDPR, KVKK, CCPA and others)

Privacy laws such as the EU/UK GDPR, Turkey's KVKK, and the California CCPA/CPRA give you rights over personal data an organisation holds about you — access, correction, deletion, portability, objection, and the right to withdraw consent. Because Odak collects and receives no personal data whatsoever, we hold nothing to access, correct, export, or delete: every one of those rights is satisfied by design, and you exercise full control directly on your device (revoke permissions in Android Settings; uninstall to erase everything). We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined in the CCPA/CPRA, and we make no automated decisions about you. If you believe we hold any data about you, or have any privacy question or complaint, email odakapp@ome-marketing.com and we will respond; you also have the right to complain to your local data-protection authority.

Children

Odak is a self-control tool intended for adults and is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your country). We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, children included — the app has no means of collecting it.

Changes to this policy

If we change how Odak handles data (for example, if a future version adds any network feature), we will update this policy, move the "Last updated" date at the top, and update the Google Play Data Safety section and any Apple privacy label before that version ships. A version of the app described by this policy will never silently start collecting data.
